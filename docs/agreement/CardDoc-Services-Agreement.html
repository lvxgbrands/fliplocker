Exhibit A

Scope of Work & Functionality List — CardDoc Platform

A-1. Platform summary

CardDoc (working title; final name developed under this Agreement) is a web-based transaction-facilitation platform for peer-to-peer trading card sales negotiated off-platform (e.g., social media). Governing business rules:

  1. CardDoc does not hold or touch the buyer's purchase money; purchase funds are released to the seller and CardDoc receives only its processing fee.
  2. CardDoc hosts no inventory and is not a marketplace or storefront; it is a pass-through verification hub.
  3. The processing fee is calculated strictly from the agreed sale price. The card's comp/market value is not collected and is never used.

A-2. Brand & identity (included)

Brand naming development (candidates, domain and basic availability screening, final selection with Client); logo design with variations; color palette and typography; brand application across platform UI and email templates.

A-3. Seller Portal

Account registration/login with email verification and password reset. Create Deal flow: upload front photo and rear photo of the card, item description, sale price, and buyer's email — creating the deal automatically alerts the buyer. Deal dashboard with live statuses. Alerts when the buyer accepts, when payment is captured (“ship now”), and at each shipping/verification step. Prepaid USPS shipping label (seller → hub) generated in the system after payment capture. Live tracking. Payout status and PayPal payout account connection.

A-4. Buyer Portal

Email invitation and account claim. Deal review page with both photos, description, sale price, and full cost breakdown. Accept and Decline buttons. PayPal checkout using authorize & capture upon acceptance. Live tracking of both shipping legs, access to the hub's unboxing video and two verification photos, and visibility of the signature-confirmed delivery event.

A-5. Payments (PayPal)

PayPal integration on the authorize & capture model. Payment structured so CardDoc receives only its processing fee, with the purchase amount disbursed to the seller automatically upon signature-confirmed delivery (PayPal multiparty/delayed-disbursement configuration, subject to PayPal's approval of Client's business account). Fee engine computes the processing fee solely from sale price, with the fee schedule configurable by Admin. Receipts and transaction records; void/refund handling for declined, cancelled, or flagged deals.

A-6. Shipping & tracking

USPS label generation via shipping API for both legs — Leg 1 (seller → hub) and Leg 2 (hub → buyer, with Signature Confirmation required). Webhook-driven tracking updates displayed on the deal timeline for both parties. Signature-confirmed delivery automatically triggers release of seller funds and the CardDoc processing fee.

A-7. Facilitator Hub Portal

Inbound package queue and check-in by tracking number. Unboxing video upload (evidence the correct card was shipped). Two still-photo uploads attached to the deal. Verification confirm step with an exception path (mismatch → deal flagged, Admin notified, refund workflow). Repack step generating the signature-required outbound label. All actions timestamped on the deal's chain-of-custody timeline.

A-8. Admin dashboard

All deals with statuses, participants, and amounts; fee schedule configuration; user management; manual overrides (cancel, refund, regenerate label, resolve exceptions); basic reporting (deal volume, fees collected).

A-9. Notifications & transparency

Automated transactional email at every lifecycle step. Every deal shows a full timestamped status timeline visible to seller and buyer: Created → Buyer Notified → Accepted → Paid → Awaiting Seller Shipment → In Transit to Hub → Received at Hub → Verified → Repacked → In Transit to Buyer → Delivered & Signed → Funds Released → Complete (plus Declined / Cancelled / Refunded / Flagged exception states).

A-10. Infrastructure & support

Responsive web application (desktop/tablet/phone browsers); HTTPS throughout; secured cloud media storage; staging and production environments; launch deployment; Terms of Service and Privacy Policy page templates. Six (6) months of post-launch technical support per Section 5 of the Agreement.

A-11. Assumptions & exclusions

  1. No card grading, authentication opinion, or valuation; hub verification is video/photographic evidence that the shipped card matches the listing.
  2. Native iOS/Android apps are out of scope; the responsive web app covers mobile use.
  3. Ongoing third-party costs are Client's responsibility per Section 4 of the Agreement.
  4. Client operates the physical hub and sets the processing-fee amounts (the system makes them configurable).
  5. PayPal's approval of multiparty/delayed-disbursement processing is a third-party dependency; if approval is pending at launch, the platform launches with a Developer-proposed interim payment configuration and switches over upon approval.
